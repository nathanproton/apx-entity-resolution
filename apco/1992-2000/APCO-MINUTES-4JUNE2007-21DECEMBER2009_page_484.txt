Dr. Boone called for any questions on the revenue that the Board may have, hearing no
comment, he began briefing the board on the budget expenditures.
Dr. Boone began with the current expenditures which is $400,000 reduction from prior year for
buses, trucks, and other equipment, $45,000 reduction in fuel costs, $175,000 reductions in
technology hardware and software, $66,000 reduction of four year old program to stated funded
level, $200,000 reduction in budgeted health care premium costs and position reductions,
$600,000 reduction in budgeted positions not filled and known retirees (18 positions), $5,000
reduction adult education program, $6,000 remove census expenditure line, $50,000 increase in
unused sick leave costs for known retirees, $179,000 increase in laurel costs, $87,000 increase in
non-salary lines to reflect increased cost (mostly electricity) and then $5,000 increase in new line
for field maintenance. Dr. Boone stated the budget expenditures include a 10 percent health
insurance premium increase and $40,000 for new health insurance subscribers.
Dr. Boone explained that other areas for further consideration would be to maintain salaries and
benefits, reduce or eliminate additional staff positions which would be 1 â€” secondary guidance
position, 3 ACHS teacher positions, 1 SBO director position, lassistant principal (Elem), reduce
all teacher contracts to 200 days and eliminate selected extracurricular stipends. Dr. Boone also
stated that some areas for consideration will be to reduce conference travel, eliminate tuition
assistance, and to reallocate unused CIP funds.
Dr. Boone explained that in the proposed FY2010 budget they are proposing to extend the
athletic director salary to a twelve month position, a new coach scale (based on new minimum
wage) and athletic field maintenance.
Dr. Boone next explained what Appomattox County spends per pupil and how we rate compared
to other school divisions.
Dr. Boone also discussed the CIP funds and again reminded the Board of the critical needs.
Dr. Boone concluded his program by stating that the FY2010 budget is a work in progress and
the next step would be to adopt the budget and then submit to the Board of Supervisors on or
about April 9, 2009,
Dr. Boone next called for any questions or concerns that the Board may have.
Mr, Craft questioned if the School Board would need to hire new teachers. Dr. Boone responded
that he anticipated having to possibly fill several positions at AMS, but his goal would be if there
are more retirees than the 18 already anticipated to not replace them.
