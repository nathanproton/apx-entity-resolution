On Monday, February 4, 2008 at 6:20 p.m. the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors
held a public hearing on proposed changes to the Ordinance for Tax Relief for the
Elderly, at the Appomattox County Administration Building, located at 153A Morton
Lane, Appomattox, Virginia.
Present: William H. Craft - Appomattox River District
Samuel E. Carter — Courthouse District
Thomas H. Conrad — Falling River District
Russell H. Moore — Piney Mountain District
Gary W. Tanner — Wreck Island District
Also Present: Aileen T. Ferguson — County Administrator
John K. Spencer ~ Assistant County Administrator
J. G, Overstreet — County Attorney
Johnnie Roark — Planner
Vicky Phelps — Administrative Assistant
Sara Henderson — Commissioner of Revenue
Chairman Craft called the public hearing to order at 6:20 p.m.
Chairman Craft explained that this public hearing would be to receive public comment on
proposed changes to Section 147-10 Exemption for Elderly and Disabled Persons in
accordance with Sections 58.1-3210 and 58.1-3506.3 Code of Virginia 1950, as amended.
Chairman Craft explained that the proposed changes would be to increase the total
combined income from $15,000 per household to $20,000 per household, and also
increase the financial worth from $90,000 to $100,000.
Chairman Craft called on Sara Henderson — Commissioner of Revenue, to give an outline
of what these changes would entail and how someone would go about qualifying for this
exemption.
Ms. Henderson explained the proposed changes and how the discount would be
calculated.
