On Monday, March 23, 2009 at 7:00 p.m. the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors held a
joint meeting with the Appomattox County School Board at the Appomattox County School
Administration Building located at 316 Court Street, Appomattox, Virginia.
Present: Thomas H. Conrad — Falling River District
Samuel E. Carter — Courthouse District
William H. Craft - Appomattox River District
Russell H. Moore — Piney Mountain District
Absent: Gary W. Tanner — Wreck Island District
Also Present: Aileen T. Ferguson — County Administrator
Vicky Phelps — Administrative Assistant
Also Present: Dr. Aldridge Boone - Superintendent
Bobby Waddell — Falling River District
Robert Mitchell - Courthouse District
Amy Martin — Piney Mountain District
Stacy Garrett —Wreck Island District
Absent: Wes Williams — Appomattox River District
Chairman Conrad called the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors to order and Robert
Mitchell called the School Board meeting to order.
Mr. Mitchell greeted the Board and the public and then turned the meeting over to Dr. Boone,
Superintendent.
Dr. Boone began with reflecting back on some successes from the past few years. Dr. Boone
stated that all Appomattox schools are accredited for six years, with three out of four schools that
met Adequate Yearly Progress and also we are one of twenty four school divisions in the
Commonwealth that met all SOL and NCLB requirements for at least two consecutive years.
Dr. Boone stated that AES is one of the 544 schools to receive the 2009 Virginia Board of
Education Excellence Award. The ACPS is one of 24 schools in the division to receive the 2009
Virginia Board of Education Excellence Award.
Dr. Boone stated thatin sports the boys and girl’s basketball and football teams went to regional
play, the boys track team won State A-Invitational and the girls placed 3°, The Band won
numerous competitions including placing five members on the District level band and one on the
State Band.
