§ 147-55 Enactment, purpose, and use
A. The County of Appomattox finds that the development and maintenance of an Enhanced 9-1-
1, or E-911 service serving the residents of the County of Appomattox and the Towns of
Appomattox and Pamplin is in the best interests of the residents and therefore intends to have
such service operating througha central office on or before July 1, 2003.
B. Under the authority of Code of Virginia § 58.1-3813.1, a special tax is enacted for the
development, installation, upgrades, and ongoing maintenance of an E-911 service. This E-
911 tax will be imposed on the consumers of telephone service or services in the County of
Appomattox and Towns of Appomattox and Pamplin.
Cc. Amounts collected from the E-911 tax shall be used solely to pay for reasonable, direct
recurring and nonrecurring (1) capital costs and operating expenses incurred by a PSAP in
designing, upgrading, leasing, purchasing, programming, installing, testing, administering,
delivering, and maintaining all necessary data, hardware, and software required to receive
and process emergency telephone calls through an E-911 system, and (2) personnel costs
(salaries, fringe benefits, and training) of dispatchers and call-takers of the E-911 system and
of the PSAP director or coordinator provided that such person has no other duties other than
the responsibility for the PSAP.
§ 147-56 Rate of E-911 tax
The rate of E-911 tax shall be fixed initially at the amount of $3.00 per month per separate telephone
access line. When the total initial capital and installation costs are paid, the E-911 tax shall be
reduced to the level necessary to offset only the recurring operation, maintenance, and personnel
costs of the E-911 system. The county treasurer shall notify the provider of the date on which the E-
911 tax is to be reduced under this section. This notification will be sent by certified mail to the
registered agent of the provider sixty (60) days in advance of the date on which the E-911 tax is to
be reduced.
§ 147-57 Exempt from E-911 tax
Access lines serving the following are exempt from the E-911 tax:
A. Federal, state, or local government agencies;
B. Public safety answering points; and
C; Any subscriber to a local telephone service who resides in a nursing home or similar adult
care facility.
