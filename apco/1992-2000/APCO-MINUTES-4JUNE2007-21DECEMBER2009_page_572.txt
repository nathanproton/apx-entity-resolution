Chairman Conrad called thé special meeting of the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors to
order at 6:00 p.m. and stated that the purpose of the Special Meeting was for the Board of
Supervisors and the Economic Development Authority to meet jointly and allow Mr. Bryan
David to provide information related to the creation of an Appomattox County Broadband
Authority.
Mr. Slagle called the EDA to order at 6:00 p.m.
Everyone joined in the Pledge of Allegiance to the American Flag.
Chairman Conrad turned the meeting over to Mr. Bryan David, Executive Director of Region
2000 Economic Development Partnership, who was assisted by Mr. Jeff Gore, an attorney with
Hefty and Wiley.
Mr. David explained that as a region, we need to move toward a technology-based economic
development program. Such a program has four components, the first of which is the
development of intellectual infrastructure that would allow us to create ideas for innovation.
This component is represented by the Center for Advanced Engineering Research (CAER),
which exists to perform real and valuable research to make our regional businesses as
competitive as possible in their various fields—particularly nuclear and wireless technology.
