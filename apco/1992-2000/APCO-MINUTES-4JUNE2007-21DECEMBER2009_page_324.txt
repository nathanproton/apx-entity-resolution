Mr. Tanner provided the Board with information from County Connections regarding the
Virginia Board of Towing and Recovery Operators under Section 46.2-2826 of the Code.
Virginia Board of Towing Recovery Operators is required to establish regulations to
Class A and Class B Operators who provide public safety towing and recovery services.
Mr. Tanner stated that smaller Class B towing operators are concerned if the new safety
tules are too strict they will be placed at significant competitive disadvantage compared
to the larger Class A operators.
Mr. Tanner stated that since residents in rural areas often rely on the smaller operators for
towing services many local governments have a stake in this issue. Mr. Tanner also
stated that being a volunteer he has a stake in this because if local operators do not
qualify, volunteers will have longer hours in traffic waiting for a larger operator and then
you are going to loose volunteers because you will be wasting their time.
Mr. Carter stated that the General Assembly has blocked the effective date until 2010.
Mr. Tanner stated that during the legislative session state police testified that if these new
regulations go into effect, 70% of the towing operators will not be qualified to perform
towing upon the request of local law enforcement agencies.
Mr. Tanner stated that he would be getting more information on this issue and bringing it
back to Board.
