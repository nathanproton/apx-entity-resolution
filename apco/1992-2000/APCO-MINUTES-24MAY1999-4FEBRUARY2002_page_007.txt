Mr. Bosiger then brought to the Board the Planning Commission Recommendations from
the May 17, 1999 public hearing.
Mr. Carwile made a motion seconded by Mr. Moore to approve petition 99-376 of
Courtland Realty Associates to rezone property from A-1 to R-2. Property is Tax Map
No. 90-A-107 and 90-A-94 and is located on Route 622 between Route 633 and Route
620. Vote: Mr. Bosiger, aye; Mr. Carter, aye, Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr.
Torrence, aye.
Mr. Torrence made a motion seconded by Mr. Moore to approve petition 99-377 of J.
Samuel Bryant, Jr. for a conditional use permit to allow storage yard and landscaping
material sales. Property is Tax Map No. 27-2-8 zoned B-1 and is located on Route 26
between Route 608 and Route 615. Vote: Mr. Bosiger, aye; Mr. Carter, aye; Mr.
Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Torrence, aye.
Mr. Bosiger then presented a request from Mr. Mike Geisinger, District Director,
Virginia Cooperative Extension, for a budget increase to their FY2000 budget to pay for
funding in order to provide Family and Consumer Science programs in the County.
Mr. Torrence made a motion seconded by Mr. Carter to transfer $800.00 from 1101-5804
to 8303-1001 in the FY2000 budget to pay for the Family and Consumer Science
programs in Appomattox County. Vote: Mr. Bosiger, aye; Mr. Carter, aye; Mr. Carwile,
aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Torrence, aye.
Mr. Bosiger then explained that it was time to name an administrative agent for the
Indoor Plumbing/Rehabilitation Program. Mr. Bosiger continued that at the present time
Lynchburg Community Action Group was handling this program for the County and that
there had been no complaints.
After discussion, and by consensus of the Board, it was agreed to have Lynchburg
Community Action Group continue to handle the Indoor Plumbing/Rehabilitation
Program for Appomattox County.
Mr. Bosiger then explained that the Appomattox County Historical Society, Inc. had
invited the members of the Board to a meeting on June 8, 1999 to discuss the plans for
the Old Jail (museum).
A lengthy discussion followed.
After discussion Mr. Carter made a motion seconded Mr. Carwile to support keeping the
wings on the museum and if necessary design the new construction around the wings.
Vote: Mr. Bosiger, aye; Mr. Carter, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr.
Torrence, aye.
