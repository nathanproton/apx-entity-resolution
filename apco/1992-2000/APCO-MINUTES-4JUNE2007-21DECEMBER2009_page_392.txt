On Monday, December 1, 2008 at 6:30 p.m. the Appomattox County Board of
Supervisors held a regular scheduled meeting at the Appomattox County Administration
Building, located at 153A Morton Lane, Appomattox, Virginia.
Present: William H. Craft — Appomattox River District
Samuel E. Carter — Courthouse District
Thomas H. Conrad — Falling River District
Russell H. Moore — Piney Mountain District
Gary W. Tanner — Wreck Island District
Also Present: Aileen T. Ferguson- County Administrator
John K. Spencer — Assistant County Administrator
J.G. Overstreet — County Attorney
Johnnie Roark — Planner
Vicky Phelps — Administrative Assistant
Chairman Craft called the regular scheduled meeting to order at 6:30 p.m. and welcomed
everyone in attendance. Chairman Craft called on Mr. Carter to deliver the invocation.
Everyone joined in the Pledge of Allegiance to the American Flag.
Chairman Craft called for the setting of the agenda. Chairman Craft added
Administrative Item #6A — Request for waiver of subdivision ordinance.
Chairman Craft then called for public comments, hearing none, he asked Mr. George
Almond, Chairman of Planning Commission to come and appear before the Board as
scheduled.
Mr. Almond first thanked Mr. Roark and his staff for the work that was done on the
citizen survey.
Mr. Almond began by stating that this was an interesting survey and began by giving a
brief history on the population of the Town and County of Appomattox.
Mr. Almond stated that this survey was viewed as an opportunity to evaluate and analyze
the rules and regulations that govern the community and gain their perception of issues.
Mr. Almond stated that from March to July 1100 surveys had been distributed through
out the county, and out of those 1100 surveys 98 were returned.
Mr. Almond explained some of the questions on the survey and how they had been
answered by the participants of the survey.
