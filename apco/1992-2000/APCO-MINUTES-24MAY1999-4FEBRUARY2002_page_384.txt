On September 5, 2000 the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors held it’s regular meeting at6:00 P.M. at the Appomattox County Courthouse, Courthouse Square, Court Street, Appomattox,Va.
PRESENT: Russell H. Moore, Chairman - Piney Mountain DistrictJerry Kendrick - Appomattox River District
Jesse Hancock - Falling River District
Samuel Carter - Courthouse District
ABSENT: Frank H. Carwile - Wreck Island District
ALSO PRESENT: Andy Carroll - Acting County Administrator
At 6:00 P.M. Mr. Moore opened the meeting and gave the invocation.
Mr. Moore called Ms. Sandra Lindsay, VDOT Resident Engineer, to address the Board. Ms. Lindsayreminded the Board of the Final Hearing on Allocation in Roanoke October 5", 2000 at theWyndham Hotel. VDOT should have a tentative plan prior to this date.
Ms. Lindsay reported on the request for a light at the Shoppes at Appomattox. She has submittedthis to the Traffic Engineer. The money for traffic signals comes through the district office. This willnot be a line item in the 6 years plan at the upcoming hearing.
Ms. Lindsay also discussed some of the Board’s concerns regarding Route 26. The District engineerwill look into these.
Ms. Lindsay discussed the Rural Addition Priorities regarding Country Haven Estates Sub-Divisionand Porter House Road. (Porter Store Road). The Board needs to draft a resolution prioritizing theseprojects. By consensus, the Board informed Mr. Carroll to draft this resolution.
Mr. Moore thanked Ms. Lindsay and called Mr. Monte Mays, Commission of Revenue to appearbefore the Board.
