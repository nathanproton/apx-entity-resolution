Chairman Craft next presented Administrative Item #6A- Waiver Request of Subdivision
Ordinance.
Chairman Craft ask Mr. Roark to appear before the Board to explain this request.
Mr. Roark explained that Mr. Bob Goodman has requested a waiver of the road frontage
requirement in the subdivision ordinance allowing him to cut off the house with several
acres and keep the bars with the farm. His intention is to develop a subdivision type of
development at a later date. Mr. Roark explained that the problem is that any way you
look at this, which is about six different ways, this can be done, however, they are all
hindrances to the future development of this property. Mr. Roark explained that the strict
application of the subdivision ordinance does seem unreasonable in this case, and
explained that this is the reason the waiver option is in the subdivision ordinance, for
unique or burdensome situations to individuals. Mr. Roark explained that the applicant
is requesting a waiver of the front requirement of the property so that he can cut off 2-3
acres and provide an easement back to State Route 707 (Peach Tree Street). Mr. Roark
stated that they have extensively studied each of the options that he had and each was
burdensome or unrealistic. Mr. Roark stated that it was decided that a waiver was his
best option.
