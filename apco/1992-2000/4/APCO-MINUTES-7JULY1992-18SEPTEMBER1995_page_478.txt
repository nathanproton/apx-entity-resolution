Mr. Torrence, Chairman, presiding, called the meeting to order at 7:00
P.M. and asked Mr. Carwile to give the invocation.
Mr. Torrence called Mr. Thomas W. Lawson, County Attorney, to appear
before the Board.
Mr. Lawson began by asking Mrs. Ferguson to read a resolution passed by
the School Board concerning the referendum on the Middle School.
Resolution as follows:
BE IT RESOLVED BY THE COUNTY SCHOOL BOARD OF APPOMATTOX COUNTY
VIRGINIA:
That as provided under Section 15.1-227-41, Code of Virginia as
amended, the School Board for Appomattox County, Virginia hereby
requests the Board of Supervisors of Appomattox County, Virginia to
adopt a resolution to take such actions as may be necessary to cause a
referendum to be held on the question of contracting a debt in the
maximum amount of SIX MILLION, SEVEN HUNDRED FIFTY THOUSAND AND 00/100
DOLLARS ($6,750,000.00) and issuing bonds to finance the cost of
purchasing land, designing, constructing, and equipping a new public
middle school building and facility in the County.
Dr. Jack P. Thomas Brenda F. Gowin
Division Superintendent Clerk of the Board
Adopted: August 15, 1994.
VOTE: Winfred Nash, aye; O. H. Tolley, Jr., aye; Richard Poe, aye;
Charles H. Booker, Jr., aye; and Samuel Scott, III, aye.
Mr. Lawson informed the Board that at this time the Board of
Supervisors would also need to adopt a resolution so that this issue
could be placed on the ballot in November 1994.
After discussion, Mrs. Beasley made a motion seconded by Mr. Carwile to
adopt the following resolution:
WHEREAS, the School Board of Appomattox County, Virginia has
adopted and filed with the Board of Supervisors of Appomattox County,
Virginia its resolution requesting the Board to take such action as may
be necessary to cause a referendum to be held on the question of
contracting a debt in the maximum amount of SIX MILLION SEVEN HUNDRED
FIFTY THOUSAND. AND 00/100 DOLLARS ($6,750,000.00) and issuing bonds for
the payment thereof for the purpose of purchasing land, designing,
constructing and equipping a new public middle school building and
facility in the County.

sense of the qualified voters of Appomattox County on the question of
