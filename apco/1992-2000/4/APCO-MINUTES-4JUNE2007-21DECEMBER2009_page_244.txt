On Monday, April 21, 2008 at 6:30 p.m. the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors
held their regular scheduled meeting at the Appomattox Community Center, located on
220 Community Lane, Appomattox, Virginia.
Present: William H. Craft - Appomattox River District
Samuel E. Carter — Courthouse District
Thomas H. Conrad — Falling River District
Russell H. Moore — Piney Mountain District
Gary W. Tanner — Wreck Island District
Also Present: Aileen T. Ferguson — County Administrator
John K. Spencer — Assistant County Administrator
J. G. Overstreet — County Attorney
Johnnie Roark — Planner
Vicky Phelps — Administrative Assistant
Chairman Craft called the meeting to order at 6:30 p.m. and called on Mr. Carter to
deliver the invocation. Everyone joined in the Pledge of Allegiance to the American Flag.
Chairman Craft welcomed everyone to the meeting and called for the setting of the
agenda. Ms. Ferguson provided additional items to be added to the consent agenda for
consideration. Mr. Tanner made a motion to approve additional items to the agenda. Mr.
Conrad seconded Mr. Tanner’s motion. Vote: Mr. Carter, aye; Mr. Conrad, aye; Mr.
Craft, aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Tanner, aye.
Chairman Craft next called for citizen public comments.
With no public comments, Chairman Craft presented Administrative Item #1- Blue &
Gray Houndsman Club. Chairman Craft called for discussion from the Board on this
item.
Mr. Tanner stated that a lot of youth are involved in hunting and this could bring revenue
into the County. Mr. Tanner made a motion to approve for the Blue & Gray Houndsman
Club to use the field adjacent to the Oakville Ruritan Club for extra parking during a
coon hunt event. Mr. Carter seconded Mr. Tanner’s motion. With no other discussion
Chairman Craft called for a vote: Mr. Carter, aye; Mr. Conrad, aye; Mr. Craft, aye; Mr.
Moore, aye; Mr. Tanner, aye.
Chairman Craft next presented Administrative Item #2- Establishment of a Central
Absentee Precinct.
