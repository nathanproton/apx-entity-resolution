Dr. Aldridge A. Boone, Superintendent, welcomed everyone to the meeting and began
with a power point presentation. Dr. Boone stated that he would like to devote the
majority of the meeting to a presentation from Blair Smith and Linda Jones from
Dominion Seven Architects to go over the Schools Capital Improvement List and then
have a question and comment session.
The Power Point presentation began with student demographic data.
The total enrollment as of June 30, 2008 was 2219, with Appomattox Primary School
enrollment being 503; Appomattox Elementary School enrollment being 530;
Appomattox Middle School enrollment being $13 and Appomattox High School
enrollment being 673. Dr. Boone stated that the projected ADM for next year is 2180.
Mr. Perrow stated that Bright Beginnings and Pre-School Handicapped are programs that
are provided by grants and we do not get ADM money for these programs.
Dr. Boone then explained that with last years 2008 graduates 163 students graduated out
of 176 eligible, with 49 of those being standard diplomas; 98 being advanced studies
diplomas; 13 being modified standard diplomas and 3 being special education diplomas.
13 seniors did not meet requirements, out of those 13 students 8 of them returned and
completed standard diploma requirements during the summer. One of those students
came back and completed advanced diploma requirements during the summer and one
student took the GED. One student completed standard diploma requirements this fall
and two of these students are currently enrolled at ACHS.
Dr. Boone also briefed the Board on the Class of 2008 Post-Secondary Plans with 137
students planning to continue in school with 87 students planning a 4 year college; 37
students planning to attend a 2 year college and 13 students planning on attending a trade,
business, or nursing school. 5 students planned to enter the military and 21 students
planned to enter the workforce.
Dr. Boone next presented the teacher demographic data with the goal being: “to have a
motivated and highly qualified teacher in every classroom.”
Dr. Boone stated that Appomattox County currently has 194 full-time teaching positions
with 27 of them being new teachers. Dr. Boone explained that Appomattox County
currently has 2 teachers not fully licensed upon employment and 82 teachers with a
Masters Degree. Appomattox County has 49 teachers with 0-4 years of experience; 39
teachers with 5-9 years of experience; 32 teachers with 10-14 years of experience; 17
teachers with 15-19 years of experience; 28 teachers with 20-24 years of experience and
29 teachers with 25 or over year’s experience.
Dr. Boone next gave a briefing on the division accomplishments by stating that all
schools are fully accredited by the Virginia Department of Education for 2007-08. the
School division and all schools, except AMS, met adequate yearly progress (AYP) under
the “No Child Left Behind Act of 2001” for 2007-08.
