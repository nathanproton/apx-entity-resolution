Mr. Carter also stated we are all aware of the unfortunate termination of jobs at Thomasville this
week due to the economy, and that we need to put forth our best efforts and to try to find
something for these individuals which will help them to stay in Appomattox and raise their
families.
Mr. Carter also questioned that if anything had come of the action items from the EDA meetings
that were held in either October or November?
Mr. Spencer stated that the EDA has scheduled a meeting early in January to look over a draft
presentation which he hopes to present to the Board during the Mid — January Board meeting.
Mr. Carter asked if there was any news on the four acre tract of land for the Museum of the
Confederacy.
Mr. Craft stated that the last time he had spoken with Ms. Ferguson on this matter; she did not
have any new information.
Mr. Craft stated that he would like to thank everyone for their cooperation and he appreciates the
Boards dedication and he would also like to wish them a Merry Christmas and a Happy New
Year.
Mr. Tanner made a motion to adjourn the meeting at 7:30 p.m. Mr. Carter seconded Mr.
Tanner’s motion. Hearing no further discussion, Chairman Craft called for a vote: Mr. Carter,
aye; Mr. Craft, aye; Mr. Conrad, aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Tanner, aye.
The meeting adjourned at 7:30 p.m.
