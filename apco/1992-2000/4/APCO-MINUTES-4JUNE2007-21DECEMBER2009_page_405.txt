Mr. Bruce Jones gave an overview of the Value-Added Beef Initiative Program. Mr. Jones
stated that the Value Added Beef Initiative Program is funded by Tobacco Commission funding
and that they receive two allocations. Mr. Jones explained that cattle are the most common
enterprise Appomattox County has.
Mr. Jones stated that a fertilizer strategy meeting was held in January 2008 and soil samples
increased 65% from 2007.
Mr. Jones told about the hay and forage field-day that was held in the summer to give citizens a
good idea of their resources.
Mr. Jones announced that Mr. Jim Smith was inducted into the County Agent Hall of Fame.
Mr. Jones briefed the Board on Agra-tourism and stated that a field day was held at the Alpaca
farm and told about the Rockcliff Farm Retreat which has pumpkin patches and blueberry
picking fields.
Mr. Jones next explained the Household Well Water Testing Program which will be conducted
May 2009. Mr. Jones explained that Appomattox/Campbell will have 350 samples maximum.
Mr. Jones also discussed the drought concerns of cattle feeding and hay and pasture land upkeep
and homeowners issues of lawn and garden and insect identification.
Mr. Jones discussed Bee Keeping as an initial education program in 2009.
Mr. Jones stated that triplet cows had been born this year with chances of this happening being
about 1 in 100-thousand.
Mr. Jones next introduced Ms. Jean Higginbotham from Amherst County.
Ms. Higginbotham stated that the Central District Supervisors had asked her to speak at this
event and to ask the Board to come in support of the State Budget Hearing which will be held in
January 2009 at CVCC.
Mr. Jones thanked the Board for their support and ended the meeting.
Chairman Craft thanked the Extension for the delicious meal that had been provided.
The meeting closed at 6:15 p.m.
