For reason of public necessity, convenience, general welfare, and good zoning practice the
Appomattox Planning Commission moves to deny the Conditional Use Permit petition of Pam
Chappell as the petitioner for the following reasons:
1. Petition does not meet the goals and objectives of the Economic Development component

2. The petition, if approved, would have a negative impact on the quality of life of the
surrounding neighborhood.
3. Project lies within a Primary Growth Area as designated by the Comprehensive Plan. A
Primary Growth Area is generally suited for agriculture and residential uses that will
gradually turn over to more densely populated residential uses, A Primary Growth Area
should be dominated by single-family residences.
