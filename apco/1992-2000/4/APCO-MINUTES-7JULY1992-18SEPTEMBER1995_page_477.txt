Mrs. Ferguson presented request from the landfill, Office on Youth, and
general properties for increases in pay to begin immediately for
several employees.
After discussion, the Board by consensus, decided that all increases
would remain effective 12/1/94.
Mrs. Ferguson read a letter from Norfolk Southern notifying the Board
of the intent to abandon 1.0 miles of rail line located in Appomattox
and Campbell Counties. After review, the Board had no objections to
this action.
Mrs. Ferguson next brought to the Board the issue of the United Way
held over from the July 1994 meeting.
After a lengthy discussion, the Board by consensus, agreed to hold this
matter for action until the September 1994 meeting.
Mrs. Ferguson informed the Board that ten (10) applications had been
received for the part-time position at the pound. Mrs. Ferguson asked
the Board for direction as to filling this position.
After discussion, and by consensus of the Board, Mrs. Ferguson was
instructed to interview and fill this position.
Being no further business, Mr. Torrence called for a motion to adjourn.
Mr. Carwile made a motion at 10:00 P.M. seconded by Mrs. Beasley to
adjourn until August 15, 1994 at 7:00 P.M. in the Board Room. Vote:
Mr. Torrence, aye; Mrs. Beasley, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye;
and Mr. Paulette, aye.
