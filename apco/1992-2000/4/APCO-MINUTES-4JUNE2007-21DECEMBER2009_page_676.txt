Mr. Overstreet stated that the County is involved in some litigation mainly because the County
was named the beneficiary in a will. Mr. Overstreet stated this is the residuary clause of a will.
A gentlemen died in 1992 and left his home and real estate to a couple and the will stated that as
long as the couple remained married and lived together on the property for fifteen years, it would
be theirs, if this did not take place the executor was to sell the property and divide among the
beneficiaries one being the Public Library Fund for Appomattox. Mr. Overstreet stated that he
had filed response on behalf of the County to the law suit and a hearing was held last week and
the County has gained ten percent of the value, but some restorations had been made by the
owner and the judge will be giving credit to the owner towards those restorations. Mr. Overstreet
stated that he would keep the Board abreast of happenings. Mr. Overstreet stated that he is
waiting on more information on restorations that have been made. He stated that he is just
reporting the pending litigation and will report again after more information is received.
