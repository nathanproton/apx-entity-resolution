Mr. Jamerson next called Mr. Lawson, Commonwealth Attorney.
Mr. Lawson informed the Board the yearly convention of the
Commonwealth Attorneys Association was scheduled for late
July in Virginia Beach and asked the Board’s prior approval
to attend. Mr. Lawson estimated his expenses to be: lodging
$196 for two (2) nights, mileage - 400 miles at 0.21/mile or
$84.00, and meals $100.00. Mr. Lawson assured the Board he
will furnish receipts for the actual expenses. Mrs. Beasley
made a motion, seconded by Mr. Carwile to approve payments
not to exceed $380.00 for expenses relating to Mr. Lawson’s
attendance at the Commonwealth Attorneys Association
Convention in Virginia Beach July 30 and 31, 1992.
Mr. Jamerson next called Mrs. Janet Belter, Treasurer. Mrs.
Belter reported a surplus as of June 30, 1992 the end of FY92
of $51,425.20. A general discussion of the County’s finances
followed. Mrs. Belter, in response to a question from Mr.
Carwile, replied she saw no problem with twice a year
billing, but would investigate further and report after she
had talked to some other County Treasurers at the upcoming
convention.
Mrs. Belter requested prior approval to attend the LGOC
(Local Government Officials Conference) in Charlottesville
August 16 and 17, 1992. Mr. Torrence made a motion seconded
by Mr. Moore to approve expenses of Treasurer Janet Belter to
attend the LGOC in Charlottesville August 16 and 17, 1992.
The expenses shall not exceed $400.00 and 1/2 of all such
expenses is to be reimbursed the County by the Compensation
Board. Vote: Mr. Jamerson, aye; Mrs. Beasley, aye; Mr.
Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Torrence, aye.
Mr. Jamerson next called Mrs. Laura McCLenny, Acting Unit
Chairman Virginia Cooperative Extension Service. The
Appomattox Extension Service is proposing to hire Mr. Kylor
Reed as an Agriculture Agent starting as early as September
1, 1992 if the Board approves the position. This would
increase the County’s share of the Extension Service’s budget
from $38,799.78 as approved for the FY93 budget to
$43,374.29. After considerable discussion, Mr. Torrence made
a motion seconded by Mr. Carwile to reject the proposal by
the Extension Service and adhere to the FY93 budget as
approved, i.e. hire an Agriculture Agent January 1, 1993 and
hire a replacement for Shirley Walton now. Vote: Mr.
Jamerson, nay; Mr. Torrence, aye; Mrs. Beasley, nay; Mr.
Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye.
