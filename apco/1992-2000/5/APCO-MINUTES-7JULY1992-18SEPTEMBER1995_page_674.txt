Mr. Torrence made a motion seconded by Mrs. Beasley to offer a letter
of support for the grant that the Chamber of Commerce is seeking to
promote tourism. Vote: Mr. Paulette, aye; Mrs. Beasley, aye; Mr.
Moore, aye; and Mr. Torrence, aye.
Mr. Paulette thanked Mr. Vaughan and the other Chamber members for the
information they provided and their efforts in the community.
Mr. Paulette next recognized Messers. Greg Winston and Wayne Bowman of
the Forestry Department.
Mr. Winston began by giving the Board of Supervisors the annual
forestry report for Appomattox County.
Mr. Winston informed the Board that on September 23, 1995 there would
be a Forest Festival in Farmville and would like for the Board members
to attend if possible. This festival is to promote forest awareness in
the area.
Mr. Winston then answered questions from Board members.
Mr. Wayne Bowman then explained that he was presenting a check to the
County in the amount of $46,934.55 which represents 1/4 of the proceeds
derived from timber sales in the State forest.
Being nothing further for Messers. Bowman and Winston they were thanked
and excused.
Mr. Paulette called for Supervisor concerns.
Mr. Torrence stated that he felt the the Board should schedule a work
session to discuss Industrial Development, prior to the end of 1995.
Mr. Torrence, also, asked what the Board had decided concerning mowing
container sites.
Mr. Moore explained that each Supervisor would mow the site in their
District.
Mr. Paulette asked Mr. Lawson, County Attorney, to check into the
section in the Decal Ordinance, that requires a portion of the decal in
order to transfer a decal.
Mr. Lawson was instructed to check into this and report back to the
Board.
Mr. Paulette next presented the first Administrative Item, committee
appointments.
Mr. Paulette asked if anyone had suggestions for appointments to the
Regional Disabilities Service Board, the Central Virginia Community
Services Board, and the Farmville VASAP Executive Board.
