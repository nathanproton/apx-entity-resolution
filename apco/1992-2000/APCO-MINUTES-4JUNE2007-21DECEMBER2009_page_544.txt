Chairman Conrad presented Administrative Item #5A ~ Resolution on the STAG Grant and
explained that this resolution would designate Mr. Johnnie Roark as the authorized
representative to transact any business and sign all documents related to the STAG Grant.
Chairman Conrad stated that staff recommends approving this resolution.
At the regular meeting of the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors on May 18, 2009, the
Board of Supervisors adopted the following Resolution for the submittal of an application to the
United States Environmental Protection Agency funding through the States and Tribal
Assistance Grants (STAG) program.
RESOLUTION
WHEREAS, the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors voted in December, 2006 to commit
to the Route 460 Water Line Project from Concord to the town limits of the Town of
Appomattox; and
WHEREAS, on January 20, 2009 the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors authorized the
County Administrator to begin engineering and design and move forward through construction
of the Route 460 Water Line; and
WHEREAS, the Route 460 Water Line Project consists of 7.5 miles of twelve (12) inch ductile
iron pipe and appurtenances along the main transportation corridor for the County; and
WHEREAS, during the most recent drought of record Appomattox County found its well water
supply for individual users strained by the impacts of the drought, thus prompting the search for
an alternative source of water; and
WHEREAS, Appomattox County and Campbell County Utilities and Service Authority are
cooperating on this project for the water source, which will be the Otter River; and
WHEREAS, the Route 460 Water Line Project will greatly enhance the quality of life for
residents along this corridor by providing a safe, reliable, and public source of water; and
WHEREAS, public safety will be greatly enhanced along the Route 460 Corridor due to
increased fire flows for established commercial businesses and making hydrants available for fire
protection to homes; and
WHEREAS, future economic development along the Route 460 Corridor will rely upon this
water source and will improve the tax base for all citizens of Appomattox County;
THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors do hereby
name and appoint Johnnie Roark, County Planner, as their authorized representative to transact
and sign any and all documents related to the securing funds from the Federal Grant awarded to
the County of Appomattox as administrated by the United States Environmental Protection
Agency.
