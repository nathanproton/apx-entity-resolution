After discussion, Mr. Torrence made a motion seconded by Mr. Moore to
adopt the following proclamation:
PROCLAMATION
NATIONAL VOCATIONAL
EDUCATION WEEK
FEBRUARY 12-18, 1995
WHEREAS, vocational education programs provide a strong foundatil
for the youth of America, our nation’s future workforce;
WHEREAS, vocational education classes aid in developing job skill
that help student to be better prepared for the technology of today’s
workplace;
WHEREAS, the vocational student organizations, DECA, FBLA, FFA,
FHA, HERO, HOSA, AND VICA, promote leadership, personal growth, and
career success among their members;
WHEREAS, these organizations perform the valuable service of
encouraging cooperation, promoting good citizenship, and inspiring
patriotism among their members;
THEREFORE, I do hereby designate the week of February 12-18, 199
as Vocational Education Week in Appomattox County.
Vote: Mr. Paulette, aye;, Mrs. Beasley, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr.
Moore, aye; and Mr. Torrence, aye.
Mr. Paulette passed out a copy of a letter from the Salvation Army
Thrift Store requesting a discount on their landfill fees.
After a lengthy discussion, and by consensus of the Board, it was
agreed to ask Patricia R. Boyer to meet with the Board of Supervisors
to discuss this request.
