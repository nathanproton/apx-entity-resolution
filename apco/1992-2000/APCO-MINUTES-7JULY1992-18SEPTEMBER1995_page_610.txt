Mr. Lawson, County Attorney, was asked if he could provide information
on VASAP.
Mr. Lawson explained that he and Mrs. Ferguson, County Administrator,
had met with Judge Spencer and the State Director of VASAP and that
VASAP has an interest in providing these services to the courts. Mr.

Mr. Lawson continued by providing information concerning the

Mr. Paulette thanked Mr. Lawson for his comments and presented the
first item under Administrative Items which was the Virginia Power
agreement.
After discussion, no action was taken.
Mr. Paulette next explained that Virginia Department of Economic
Development was asking for a proclamation promoting May 21-27, 1995 as
Industry Appreciation Week.
Mr. Torrence made a motion seconded by Mrs. Beasley to adopt the
following proclamation:
PROCLAMATION
INDUSTRY APPRECIATION WEEK IN VIRGINIA
WHEREAS, Appomattox County is blessed with a strong manufacturing
base; and
WHEREAS, these industries provide much needed jobs for the
citizens of Appomattox County; and
WHEREAS, these same industries provide local taxes from which the
entire local citizenry benefit; and
WHEREAS, we recognize and appreciate these important facts;
NOW, THEREFORE, we the Board of Supervisors of Apppomattox County
hereby salutes our existing industry base, and by virtue of this
proclamation give notice to our citizens that Virginia Industries Are
Building A Better Commonwealth;
AND, FURTHER, that the Week of May 21-27, 1995, is Industry
Appreciation Week in Appomattox County.
Vote: Mr. Paulette, aye; Mrs. Beasley, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr.
Moore, aye; and Mr. Torrence, aye.
Mr. Paulette presented a request from Central Virginia Planning
District Commission for Appomattox County to participate in a household
hazardous waste collection program with surrounding localities.
After discussion, no action was taken.
