Mr. Carroll pointed out that last year (FY2001) the total adopted budget was $23,579,112. The
proposed for this year (FY2002) is $24,237,311 which an increase of $658,000 or 2.79% increase.
Mr. Kendrick asked about the revenue estimates for the upcoming year.
Mr. Carroll reported that revenues have dropped $23,000 and some of that is because last year we
transferred in $700,000 from the IDA Bond for different Capitol Improvements and this year we
have $280,000 coming in from either the IDA Bond or Capitol Improvement, etc. with a net negative
change of $23,620. Which equates to $616,337 short.

Need to keep in mind that this note will be refinanced, hence the increase in 2003.
