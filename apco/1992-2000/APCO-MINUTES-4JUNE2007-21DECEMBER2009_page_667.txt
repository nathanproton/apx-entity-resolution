Dr. Grasty explained the 4-Year Old at Risk Program which is called Bright Beginnings and
stated that this is a very valuable program that is housed at the Appomattox Primary School. She
explained that there are three classes of eighteen students (54 total) with three teachers and three
aides. Dr. Grasty explained that these students do not count towards the ADM.
Dr. Grasty next discussed the total amount of students enrolled in Career and Technical
Education (CTE) Completers for 2009. This program includes Agriculture Machinery;
Agriculture Production; Horticulture Science; Veterinary Science; Floral Design; Business;
EMT; Nursing Assistant; General Marketing: Drafting; Robotics; Automotive Services; Early
Childhood; Family and Human Services; Culinary Arts.
Dr. Grasty next discussed the required staff for Special Education and explained that there are a
total of 19-teachers for Special Education; eight instructional aides for Special Education; one
school psychologist; one sign language interpreter; Dr. Grasty also stated that they also employ
an occupational therapist, physical therapist; speech therapist; vision instructor; and an autism
specialist and then stated that these staff serve 315 students as required by federal and state law.
Dr. Grasty explained that they currently have 188 full-time teaching positions; 22-new teachers;
75 —teachers that hold a masters degree. Dr. Grasty explained that the attrition rate for teacher for
08-09 was 12.5%. Dr. Grasty also stated that thirteen teachers retired last year and twelve
teachers resigned. Dr. Grasty explained that Appomattox County Schools have 58 teachers with
0-4 years experience; 40-teachers with 5-9 years experience; 29-teachers with 10-14 years
experience; 18-teachers with 15-19 years experience; 14-teachers with 20-24 years experience;
and 29-teachers with 25 or more years experience.
In other staff demographics Dr. Grasty explained that there are currently 46- bus drivers; 3-
crossing guards; 2- mechanics; 18-custodians; 24-clerical; 4-maintenance; 4-nurses; 39-
instructional assistants; 4-principals; 5-assistant principals; 3-directors; and 4- supervisors.
Next Dr. Grasty discussed the accomplishments for each of the schools.
The accomplishments for Appomattox Elementary School being that they are fully accredited
and met AYP; Awarded National Distinguished Title I School; Awarded Virginia Board of
Education Excellence Award; 95% proficiency average on SOL’s; Professional Partnership with
Longwood University; Increased writing scores by ten points between 2007-2008 and 2008-

The accomplishments of the Appomattox Primary School being fully accredited and met AYP;
