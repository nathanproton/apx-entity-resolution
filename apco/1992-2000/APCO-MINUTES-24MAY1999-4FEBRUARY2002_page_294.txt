Mr. Brian Selz, came forward, gave his address as Courthouse Square, Rustburg, Va. Mr. Selz stated
he was there on behalf of some of the residents of Station Lane. Mr. Selz passed around a diagram,
part of the tax map of the area in question, the green is used for residential, the blue for areas used
for agriculture and the remaining area is in Campbell County. He pointed out a couple of things of
a technical nature. The industrial use in this part of Concord has no relation to comprehensive plan
and no logical planning reason to do this, just the opposite for the planning of Concord area. Anyone
who knows this area knows that for the past 12 years or so this has been zoned agriculture and
nothing has changed. If any change has taken place it has been more toward agriculture and less
toward any thing else. The problem has not been just with the sandblasting, the problem has been
with the parking of trailers, the dumping of chicken manure and various other things that the County
Government does not allow. Mr. Moore came before the Board last summer. He had received a
letter citing some of the zoning discrepancies to which Mr. Moore did not appeal, he let that decision
stand. He came to the Board and asked the Board to help him declare that the use he wanted had
been grand-fathered in. There has been a non-conforming use for the last several years. The purpose
of non-conforming use is as the old time uses fade out, the property is zoned for new uses.
