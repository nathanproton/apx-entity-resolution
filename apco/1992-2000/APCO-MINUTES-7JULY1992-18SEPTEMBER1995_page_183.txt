Mr. Jamerson next asked the Board to consider a request from Stonewall
Vineyards asking the permit fee to be waived for a proposed block dance
May 15, 1993. After review and discussion, Mr. Torrence made a motion
seconded by Mr. Carwile to approve the permit and waive the permit
fee. Vote: Mr. Jamerson, aye; Mrs. Beasley, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye;
Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Torrence, aye.
Mr. Jamerson next asked Mr. Lawson the status of the transfer of the
Town’s ballpark to the County. Mr. Lawson reported he would soon have
the deed completed and ready to record.
Mr. Rice reported the Treasurer asked for guidance on long term
investing of Landfill Bond proceeds. After discussion, the Board asked
Mr. Rice to determine the current rates and prepare a payment schedule
of the committed money.
Mr. Rice also reported, Mr. Freddie Godsey had informed him he would
accept the position of Emergency Services Coordinator, but would expect
some monetary compensation for the extra work as had been the custom in
the past. Mr. Rice also reported the State Emergency Services office
had informed the County an employee paid by the Compensation Board
would not be eligible for additional state reimbursement as an
Emergency Services Coordinator. After considerable discussion, the
Board by consensus asked Mr. Godsey to attend the Emergency Planning
Course sponsored by the State Department of Emergency Services May
25-27 in Roanoke and report to the Board at their June 14, 1993 meeting.
Being no further business, Mrs. Beasley made a motion seconded by Mr.
Carwile to adjourn at 9:40 P.M. Vote: Mr. Jamerson, aye; Mrs.
Beasley, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Torrence, aye.
