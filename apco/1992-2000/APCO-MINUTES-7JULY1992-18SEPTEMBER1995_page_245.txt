Mr. Jamerson also brought up a request from Mr. Peterson to pay
membership fee to the Solid Waste Association of North America. Mr.
Carwile made a motion seconded by Mr. Torrence to pay the $100.00
membership fee for the Solid Waste Association of North America. Vote:
Mr. Jamerson, aye; Mrs. Beasley, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye
and Mr. Torrence, aye.
Being no further business with Mr. Peterson, Mr. Jamerson thanked and
excused Mr. Peterson and called Dr. Thomas, Superintendent of Schools
to appear before the Board. Dr. Thomas’ first item for discussion was
the School Board’s concern over the use of the Literacy Fund. Dr.
Thomas solicites the Board’s help in writing letters to Delegate Abbitt
and Senator Holland expressing the Boards concerns. After discussion,
Mr. Torrence made a motion seconded by Mrs. Beasley to write letters to
Delegate Abbitt and Senator Holland expressing the Board’s concerns
over the status of the Literacy Fund and requesting their support in
keeping this Fund intact. Vote: Mr. Jamerson, aye; Mrs. Beasley, aye;
Mr. Carwile, aye; Mr. Moore, aye and Mr. Torrence, aye.
Dr. Thomas next presented the Board with a handout showing financing
scenarios from Virginia Public School Authority for the Middle School
Construction project. The following financing scenarios were used:
Scenario #1 Fall 1993 @ $4,660,000 @6.50% 20 years.
Scenario #2 Fall 1993 @ $6,160,000 @6.50% 20 years.
Scenario #3 Fall 1993 @ $6,475,000 @6.50% 20 years.
Scenario #4 Fall 1993 @ $7,975,000 @6.50% 20 years.
Dr. Thomas indicated that these were "worse Case" scenarios.
After a lengthy discussion, the Board requested Dr. Thomas schedule a
meeting with the School Board and Board of Supervisors to set down and
go over these scenarios on August 25, 1993 at 6:00 P.M. prior to the
School Board meeting. Being nothing further for Dr. Thomas, Mr.
Jamerson thanked and excused him.
Mr. Jamerson next asked Mrs. Ferguson to go over the Administrative
items beginning with a request from Fred Crowe for information as to
who has charge of the ballpark. The Recreation Advisory Committee
would like to see the ballpark put under the supervision of the
Recreation Director.
After discussion the Board requested Mrs. Ferguson ask Mrs. Lawson,
Recreation Director to appear before the Board at the September meeting
with proposed fee structure for use of the ball park and operation
procedures.
