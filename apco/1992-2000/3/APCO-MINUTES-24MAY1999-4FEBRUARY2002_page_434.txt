§ 147-59 Delinquent Taxes
Any consumer failing to pay, or any provider failing to collect and remit, such E-911 tax on the
month that it is due shall incur a penalty of 10% of the amount due plus interest at the same rate as
the percentage interest paid to the taxpayer by the County for overpayments due to erroneously
assessed taxes or at the maximum rate permitted under § 58.1- 3916 of the Code of Virginia as
amended, whichever amount is higher. In addition to any penalty and interest owed, the consumer
or provider will be responsible for the costs of attorneys' fees and or collection agency fees in an
amount not exceed twenty percent of the delinquent taxes and other charges collected.
§ 147-60 Records
Providers shall keep complete records showing all purchases of local telephone services to
consumers in the County of Appomattox and Towns of Appomattox and Pamplin, which records
shall show the date of service, the date of billing, the date of payment thereof, and the amount of E-
911 tax imposed hereunder. Such records shall be kept at the provider’s offices for a period of three
years and shall be made available for inspection by the duly authorized agents of the governing body
at reasonable times. Copies of such records shall be provided to the County upon request.
§ 147-61 Receipt and disbursement by Treasurer
The E-911 tax collected pursuant to this ordinance is solely for the initial capital, installation, and
ongoing maintenance costs of the E-911 system. E-911 taxes collected from consumers shall be
deemed to be held in trust by the provider until remitted to the Treasurer of the County of
Appomattox. The Treasurer of the County of Appomattox shall deposit all E-911 taxes collected and
remitted from providers into a separate E-911 revenue fund with accounting of such funds to be used
solely for the purposes authorized by this ordinance.
§ 147-62 Compensation to providers
Any provider of local telephone service that collects this E-911 tax shall be compensated in the
amount of three percent of the amount of E-911 tax due and accounted for in the form of a deduction
in submitting the return and paying the amount due by it.
§ 147-63 Effective date
This ordinance shall be effective 120 days after certified written notice of its enactment is received
by the registered agent of the service providers required to collect the E-911 taxes.
