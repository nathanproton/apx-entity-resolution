INDEMNIFICATION AND COMMUNITY REVITALIZATION COMMISSION
EDUCATION GRANT PROGRAM FUNDS TO ADDRESS WORKFORCE
DEVELOPMENT AND EDUCATION NEEDS IN THE COUNT OF APPOMATTOX
WHEREAS, The Virginia Tobacco Indemnification and Community Revitalization Commission
(TICRC) has announced available funding for their Education Grant Program, and
WHEREAS, The County of Appomattox and the Appomattox Workforce Development Center
Management Team underwent a thorough planning process to identify and prioritize long-term needs to
strengthen and sustain the County of Appomattox, has identified workforce training deficiencies and
opportunities in the county; and
RESOLUTION TO ENDORSE THE APPLICATION FOR VIRGINIA TOBACCO
WHEREAS, The County of Appomattox, acting on the recommendations developed in the
abovementioned process, has developed and opened Phase I of the Appomattox Workforce Development
Center at the Carver-Price Educational Complex; and
WHEREAS, due to outstanding community interest and support, the County of Appomattox and
Central Virginia Community College have agreed to continue the execution of the Appomattox Workforce
Development Center Master Plan by initiating the construction of Phase II, which will include additional
classroom and laboratory space; and
WHEREAS, approximately $700,950 will be expended on Phase II of this project, with the
primary funding sources being a TICRC Education grant ($250,000 awarded in 2006 and an anticipated
award of $250,000 in 2007), an anticipated TICRC Economic Development Grant of $71,401 and $129,549
from the County of Appomattox and other potential sources; and
WHEREAS, the Appomattox County Board of Supervisors is committed to the continued
development and expansion of a workforce development center in Appomattox and will be responsible for
the management, maintenance, and operation of the building to ensure that the center can continue as long
as Central Virginia Community College continues to lease the facility for classes and programs that
improve and enhance the education and career preparedness level of the workforce and business
community of Appomattox County.
