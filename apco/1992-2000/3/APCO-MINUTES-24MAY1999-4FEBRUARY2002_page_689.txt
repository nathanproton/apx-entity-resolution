The Appomattox County Board of Supervisors held a Public Hearing on Monday, February 4,
2002 at 6:30 P.M. to receive comments on the replacing of the County’s Welcome (Gateway)
Signs.
PRESENT: Samuel E. Carter, Chairman - Courthouse District
Jesse L. Hancock - Falling River District
Frank H. Carwile - Wreck Island District
Jerry G. Kendrick - Appomattox River District
ABSENT: Russell H. Moore - Piney Mountain District
(Arrived at 6:33 P.M.)
ALSO PRESENT:
Andy Carroll - County Administrator
John Spencer - Assistant County Administrator
Lee Byrd - Attorney - Sands, Anderson, Marks & Miller
Dan Siegel - Attorney - Sands, Anderson, Marks & Miller
At 6:33 P.M. Mr. Carter opened the public hearing and stated the purpose of the hearing was to
receive comments regarding the County’s entrance/gateway signs. Mr. Carter also asked that
each speaker come forward and give their name and address for the record.
Mr. Carter called the first speaker, Mrs. Edith McCormick.
Mrs. McCormick gave her address as Spout Spring. She stated she had written and also spoken
to the Board of Supervisors regarding not replacing the present signs. Please do not purchase the
new signs. Mrs. McCormick also presented the Board with a petition containing approximately
290 names of citizens against the new signs. She also feels we should use the money originally
intended for signs to upkeep and beautify the original signs.
