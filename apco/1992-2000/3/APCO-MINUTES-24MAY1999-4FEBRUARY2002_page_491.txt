Ms. Harrup first thanked the Board for their contribution to her organization this year. She stated
they had relocated into a 73,000 square foot building in April. They have not had a formal ribbon
cutting because they are very frugal with their funds. They will be celebrating their 75"
Anniversary later this year and they will roll these into one event and everyone will be invited.
Ms. Harrup gave brief summary of what STEPS is all about. They are very proud to say that
Appomattox has reduced their welfare roll due to the efforts of the staff at STEPS. She is submitting
a letter of request for funding for the upcoming fiscal year and she has broken out where their
funding comes from because she feels it is important for the Board to see that they are trying to earn
their own way as well as coming to the various counties that they service for contributions. Last year
68% of their entire operating budget and their revenue source was generated from production related
activities. She feels they are making vast progress.
Ms. Harrup stated that of the individuals they served last year, five (5) citizens of Appomattox were
served. They operate a van that comes to Appomattox County to pick up individuals that are in need
of their services and eight (8) people from Social Services also received services last year. They also
work closely with the school system with special education students. Ms. Harrup invited everyone
to come by their manufacturing facility. They are located in the old Craddock-Terry factory. Every
individual who comes to their facility receives a pay check. The Farmville operation paid $530,000
in wages last year. 75% of their direct labor is provided by persons with disabilities.
Their biggest constraint at this time is Transportation.
