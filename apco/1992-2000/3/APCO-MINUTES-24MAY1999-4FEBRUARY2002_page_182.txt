At a regularly scheduled meeting of the Board of Supervisors for the County of
Appomattox held on the 10" day of January, 2000, on motion of Supervisor Carter,
seconded by Supervisor Carwile the following resolution was adopted:
WHEREAS, in the opinion of the Board of Supervisors of Appomattox County, itisa
matter of public necessity that the County improve a portion of Highway 26, being
adjacent to the northbound lane of travel 751.90 feet from its intersection with State
Route 1029, along Route 26, 479.39 feet, on property owned by Kathleen G. Stevens, et
als; and
WHEREAS, the Industrial Development Authority of Appomattox County has purchased
property directly across Route 26 from the above described property and is in the process
of constructing an entrance to said property;
WHEREAS, the Industrial Development Authority will construct roads, buildings and new
business for Appomattox County, and will provide jobs for Appomattox County citizens;
and
WHEREAS, the property upon which the Industrial Park is to be located has no industrial
access road to Route 26 and requires construction of an industrial road from the Industrial
Development Authority property into Route 26.
WHEREAS, as an incentive to the Virginia Department of Transportation to build said
roadway, this board resolved to request industrial access road funding from said
Department and agreed that in exchange for that funding, it would be at no cost to the
Virginia Department of Transportation guarantee necessary rights of way and that the new
roadway so constructed would be added to and become a part of the Primary and
Secondary System of Highways for the County of Appomattox, and
WHEREAS, Kathleen G. Stevens, et als are owners of a small parcel of land which is
necessary to the construction of this access road; and
WHEREAS, construction of an industrial access road is a public purpose justifying the
exercise of the power or eminent domain, and
