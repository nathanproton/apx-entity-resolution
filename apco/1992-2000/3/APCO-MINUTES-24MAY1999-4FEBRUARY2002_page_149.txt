Mr. Austin began by introducing Ms. Sandra Lindsay, VDOT Resident Engineer, Mr.
Mike Crawford, Maintenance Operations Manager, and Mr. Robert Brown, Contract
Administrator.
Mr. Austin then gave a presentation explaining the proposed Six-Year Plan.
Mr. Austin then called for questions concerning his presentation.
Mr. Carwile asked if the traffic count was the controlling factor for the unpaved roads.
Mr. Austin answered that you would need a traffic count of at least 50 to determine
whether it could be paved.
Mr. Austin called for further questions on the Six-Year Plan.
Hearing none, Mr. Austin moved to the Budget for the secondary road system and gave a
brief explanation of the proposed budget.
Mr. Austin called for questions concerning the budget.
Mr. Carwile asked a question concerning the tentative allocation for the coming years
budget.
Mr. Austin and Ms. Lindsay explained that there was no way of knowing whether the
allocation will increase or decrease, but the budget was usually pretty close.
Mr. Austin then called for requests for improvements or new additions to the secondary
road system.
No improvements were requested.
Mr. Philip Jones came forward and gave his address as 438 Walnut Lane, Lynchburg,
Virginia 24501. Mr. Jones continued that he was speaking on behalf of Country Haven
Estates. Mr. Jones asked that the remaining sections of road in Country Haven Estates,
on the Appomattox side, be taken into the 50/50 State program. Mr. Jones explained that
approximately 11/2 mile of Main Street and three side streets of approximately 1 mile
also be taken into the 50/50 program.
Mr. Carwile asked Mr. Jones if this was for the Appomattox side?
Mr. Jones stated that it was.
Mr. Austin asked Mr. Jones if he were familiar with the process?
Mr. Jones stated that he was.
