A motion was made by Mr. Carter, seconded by Mr. Kendrick to hold a Public Hearing on July 3,
2000 at 6:00 P.M. to receive comments on the Proposed Ordinance Changes to the Code. VOTE:
Mr. Moore, aye; Mr. Kendrick, aye; Mr. Hancock, aye; Mr. Carwile, aye; and Mr. Carter, aye.
Mr. Carroll was instructed to advertise this Public Hearing for two (2) weeks prior to July 3.
The Board of Supervisors set June 15th as the date to interview candidates for the Building &
Housing Inspector and Landfill Supervisor positions.
Mr. Kendrick made a motion seconded by Mr. Carter to restructure our organization to avoid any
potential legal conflicts in hiring a new County Attorney. Mr. Moore called for discussion.
Mr. Carwile stated that this motion is hinged on a legal issue dealing with the Commonwealth
Attorney and he cannot and will not support it.
Mr. Hancock stated he could not support this motion at this time.
Mr. Moore then called for a vote on the aforementioned motion. VOTE: Mr. Moore, aye; Mr.
Kendrick, aye; Mr. Hancock, nay; Mr. Carwile, nay; and Mr. Carter, aye.
Mr. Moore stated that a motion had been made and carried that the Board of Supervisors hire a new
County Attorney.
