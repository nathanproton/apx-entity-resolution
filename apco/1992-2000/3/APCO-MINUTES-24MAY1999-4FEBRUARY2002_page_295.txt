Mr. Moore asked if anyone else wished to speak against this petitions.
Mr. James W. Franklin came forward and stated he has been a resident of Concord for 68 years,
other that the time he spent in the service, he has spent his life in the village of Concord. He is very
dedicated to it and enjoys living there. Over the years, things change and changes are inevitable.
Zoning is necessary to give some sense of direction between commercial and residential property.
It has served Concord well, because with all the changes, it is still a quiet, peaceful place. Concord
historically and sociologically deserves better than to become a tractor trailer parking lot/graveyard.
We need to preserve those things that are of value to the community.
