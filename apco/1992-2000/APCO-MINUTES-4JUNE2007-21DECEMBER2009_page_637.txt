Chairman Conrad called for Public Comment, hearing no public comment; Chairman Conrad
called on Mr. Mike Womack, Forest Management Specialist to appear before the Board to give
his annual report and also to present the Board with a check for the Countyâ€™s share of revenues

Mr. Womack came forward and stated that historically the State Forest gives 25% back to the
County, but the General Assembly has now changed that to 12.5% keeping the other half and
using the funds for enhancement of the recreation activities in the State Forest.
Mr. Womack stated that in the past year they have used the money for enhancing recreation by
cutting back the brush for hiking trails. They have also created some new maps and brochures of
the recreational areas in the State Forest. Mr. Womack stated that they have also constructed day
use areas which provide parking for horse trailers.
Mr. Womack stated that during the last General Assembly Session they enacted a change for the
State Park this year which is a user permit and it is going to require you to have a permit not only
for hunting, but to trap, fish, mountain bike ride and horse ride on the State Park which will be
state wide.
Mr. Womack called for questions or comments from the Board.
Mr. Carter stated that he had someone tell him about an abundance of their hardwood trees dying
and questioned if they had noticed the same in the State Park.
Mr. Womack stated that he has not noted any of that in the State Park and it must be an isolated
area that this is happening in. Mr. Womack stated that if this citizen wanted someone to come
and look at his trees that he could contact Rick Butler at the Appomattox Forestry Office.
Mr. Womack called for other questions or concerns. Hearing none; he presented the Board with a
check in the amount of $23,490.35.
Mr. Conrad thanked Mr. Womack for his annual report and also for the check in the amount of
$23,490.35.
